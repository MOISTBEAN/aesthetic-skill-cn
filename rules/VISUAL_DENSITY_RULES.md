# Visual Density Rules

Use these rules for desktop proof screenshots at `1440 × 1200` and adapt the same hierarchy responsively.

## Required composition

- one clear header or sidebar identifying context
- at least three distinct information zones
- one dominant primary workflow area
- one secondary detail or evidence panel
- one table, timeline, queue, calendar, editor, or task list
- at least one warning, pending, failed, changed, needs-review, or risk state
- visible next action tied to the exception

## Hard failures

- hero-only empty layout
- slogan text occupying more than 30% of the viewport without workflow content
- first viewport dominated by equal cards
- large blank area caused by a narrow top-left demo
- chart used as the primary visual without a comparison question
- density created through nested card borders instead of alignment and grouping

## Density method

1. Place the primary object and urgent exception first.
2. Use rows and columns for comparable facts.
3. Keep numbers aligned and units visible.
4. Move evidence and history into a stable secondary panel.
5. Use spacing or one divider to group content; do not box every label.
6. At mobile widths, stack workflow before detail and allow dense tables to scroll horizontally.

The screenshot must feel occupied by useful product structure, not stretched decoration.
